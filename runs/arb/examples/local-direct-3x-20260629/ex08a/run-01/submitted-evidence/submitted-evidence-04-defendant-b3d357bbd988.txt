Business Insider article title: "Jeffrey Epstein's brother says he thinks newly surfaced 'suicide note' is a forgery." Author: Jacob Shamsian. Publication timestamp shown: 2026-05-08T20:44:17.764Z.

Relevant extract: The article reports that Mark Epstein, Jeffrey Epstein's brother, saw the purported suicide note and did not believe it was real. It reports that Mark Epstein, who has long maintained his brother was murdered, told Business Insider the note unsealed that week was a forgery. The article quotes Mark Epstein as saying: "It wouldn't be hard to get some pro forger to forge a note." It then reports that Nicholas Tartaglione, Epstein's former cellmate, claimed to have found the note; that the note was not written immediately before Epstein's August 10, 2019 death but during an earlier July incident according to Tartaglione; and that Tartaglione's lawyers entered the note into his criminal case, where it was sealed in a dispute over legal representation until Judge Kenneth Karas unsealed it.

The article reports that the note uses a line similar to language in Epstein emails later made public by DOJ, including the "Watcha want me to do" / "Bust out cryin" phrasing, which Mark Epstein said could have been copied to mimic his brother's voice. The article also notes the contrary point that, according to Judge Karas's unsealing order, the note was originally sealed in May 2021 before the emails with the Little Rascals reference became public in 2026, and that a timeline in the Epstein files showed the note passed through multiple people who tried to authenticate it in 2019 and 2020.

Further adverse points: The article reports that the note itself was not included in the Justice Department's Epstein files, nor in a 128-page Justice Department inspector general report following four years of investigation into Epstein's death. It also reports that Epstein's first reported suicide attempt was disputed: he was found with a homemade noose around his neck according to an internal Bureau of Prisons report; Tartaglione's lawyers called it a suicide attempt; Epstein initially told jail officials Tartaglione tried to kill him and later said he was extremely tired and did not remember it well. Mark Epstein told Business Insider he doubted Tartaglione had or found the note and argued that, if Jeffrey Epstein had chosen to kill himself, he would not have written a note.
