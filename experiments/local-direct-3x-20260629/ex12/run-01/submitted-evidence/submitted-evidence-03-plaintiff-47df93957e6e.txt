- Article title: Iran Restricts Airspace as U.S. Signals Imminent Strike Window.
- Author/date: by Jason Ndunyu, May 23, 2026.
- The article states that Iran closed portions of its airspace as reports emerged that the United States was preparing for potential military strikes while maintaining diplomatic signaling with Tehran.
- It states that, according to a newly issued Notice to Air Missions (NOTAM), identified as OIIX A1010/26, all airports in the western portion of the Tehran Flight Information Region were closed to operations except for a limited number of facilities operating only during daylight hours.
- It states that the restriction was effective from Friday, May 22 at 20:46Z until Monday, May 25 at 08:30Z, applied broadly to civil aviation activity, and permitted only designated airports to operate between sunrise and sunset under strict conditions.
- It states that, according to the NOTAM, all prior operator permissions were suspended and new approvals had to be obtained from Iran's Civil Aviation Authority for IFR passenger operations at the affected airports.
- It states that the measure affected key regional airports while maintaining limited daytime activity at selected sites, including OITR, OIKK, OIAA, OISS, OIYY, OICC, OIGG, and OIAW.
- It states that, as of May 23, 2026, live Flightradar24 tracking showed a notable disruption in civilian air traffic over and around Iran following reports of the NOTAM, with visible flight paths bending around western, central, and eastern corridors and most aircraft diverting north, south, or further east.
- It attributes the timing of the restriction to U.S. military and intelligence officials adjusting posture in anticipation of possible developments involving Iran; it describes CBS reporting that the administration was preparing for a fresh round of military strikes but no final decision had been made as of Friday afternoon.
