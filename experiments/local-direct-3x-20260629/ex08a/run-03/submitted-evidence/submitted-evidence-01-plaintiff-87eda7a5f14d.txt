UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT, SOUTHERN DISTRICT OF NEW YORK
United States of America v. Nicholas Tartaglione, Case No. 16-CR-832 (KMK)
ORDER, Kenneth M. Karas, United States District Judge, dated May 6, 2026, White Plains, New York.

Relevant extracted text:

"Non-Party newspaper, the New York Times (the 'Times'), has filed a request to unseal certain materials that, the Times claims, were filed in this case. ... For reasons set forth below, the Court determines that the Times' request to unseal an alleged 'suicide note purportedly authored by Jeffrey Epstein, recovered by ... Tartaglione, and then reportedly submitted by ... Tartaglione's attorneys in this case (the 'Note'),' ... should be granted. The Court reserves decision on the remainder of the Times' requests to unseal."

The order states that, while Nicholas Tartaglione was awaiting trial, "he was briefly cellmates with Jeffrey Epstein at the Metropolitan Correctional Center in Manhattan."

The order states that on April 30, 2026, the Times sought access to four sets of documents, including: "A suicide note purportedly authored by Jeffrey Epstein, recovered by Mr. Tartaglione, and then reportedly submitted by Mr. Tartaglione's attorneys in this case (the 'Note')."

The order states that the Times asserted Tartaglione had repeatedly referenced the Note in interviews and that a Department of Justice document referenced the Note. According to the order's summary of the Times' position, "the Note predated Jeffrey Epstein's death by over a week, and predated Epstein being placed in a different cell from Defendant."

The order states that the Government submitted a responsive letter indicating that it was "unaware of the subject matter of the Curcio proceedings" beyond what appeared publicly on the docket.

The order states that Tartaglione's co-counsel submitted a sealed letter and that "Defendant took the position that any information Defendant had shared about the Note with third parties was no longer privileged, and therefore he did not object to the Note being unsealed."

In the discussion of public access, the order states that public access to the Note promotes accountability and confidence in the administration of justice. It also states that the court considered the privacy interests of third parties, including Epstein, and concluded those interests did not support continued sealing.

Conclusion: "For the reasons set forth above, the Times' request is granted as to the Note. The Clerk of Court is respectfully directed to docket a copy of the material that was placed in the vault on May 20, 2021, and to unseal the original material placed in the vault on May 20, 2021. The Court reserves decision as to the remainder of the materials requested by the Times."
