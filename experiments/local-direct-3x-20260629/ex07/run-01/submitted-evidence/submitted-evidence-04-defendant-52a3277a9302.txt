Source: The New Voice of Ukraine, "ISW says Russia used ceasefire to reinforce positions," 10 May 2026, 09:30 AM. Accessible page capture via web.run on 2026-06-30.

Relevant captured content: NV reports that Russian and Ukrainian forces continued conducting offensive operations across the frontline during the first day of the May 9-11 ceasefire, although fighting intensity decreased, citing the Institute for the Study of War's May 9 assessment. NV states Ukraine's General Staff reported 51 combat engagements along the frontline on May 9 and that Russian forces continued strikes against Kharkiv Oblast and Kherson Oblast. NV also reports Russia's Defense Ministry claimed Ukrainian forces committed 8,970 ceasefire violations.

NV further reports that Ukraine/Russia reports and NASA monitoring data indicated combat activity on May 9, especially in northern Ukraine, declined but did not stop completely. It says ISW noted the ceasefire lacked clearly defined conditions, and that the only publicly known detail was that Ukraine would refrain from striking Red Square during the Victory Day parade, a condition Kyiv upheld. NV states ISW concluded mutual accusations and continued localized fighting on May 9 showed that a ceasefire lacking clear mechanisms, reliable third-party monitoring, dispute resolution, and troop withdrawals was unlikely to be sustainable.

NV also reports ISW found Russian forces used the ceasefire period for rotations, reinforcements, redeployment, and logistics improvements across the frontline in preparation for future offensives, and expected Russian forces to continue regrouping on May 10 and 11. The article notes President Trump announced the May 9-11 ceasefire on May 8 and that President Zelenskyy later confirmed the agreement and planned 1,000-for-1,000 prisoner exchange.
