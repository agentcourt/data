Source: PBS NewsHour / Associated Press, A timeline of U.S. military escalation against Venezuela leading to Maduro's capture. By Ben Finley, Konstantin Toropin, Regina Garcia Cano. Date on page: Jan. 3, 2026 1:54 PM EDT. URL: https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/a-timeline-of-u-s-military-escalation-against-venezuela-leading-to-maduros-capture

Extracted relevant page text:
- AP reported that Trump had threatened military strikes on targets on Venezuelan territory after months of attacks on boats accused of carrying drugs from Venezuela; Maduro said U.S. military operations were a thinly veiled effort to oust him.
- On Jan. 3 the U.S. conducted a large-scale strike against Venezuela. Maduro and Cilia Flores were taken from their home on a military base and were aboard a U.S. warship on their way to New York to face criminal charges.
- Before the escalation, there had been 35 known strikes against alleged drug-smuggling boats in South American waters since early September, killing at least 115 people according to administration announcements.
- The U.S. sent a fleet of warships to the region, the largest buildup of forces in generations; the naval force grew to include guided-missile destroyers, three amphibious assault ships and other vessels, about 6,000 sailors and Marines, aircraft, F-35 fighter jets in Puerto Rico, and a Navy submarine carrying cruise missiles off South America.
- On Oct. 15, Trump confirmed he had authorized the CIA to conduct covert operations inside Venezuela and was weighing land operations in the country.
- On Dec. 29/30, a drone strike at a docking area believed used by Venezuelan drug cartels was reported as the first known direct operation on Venezuelan soil since the strikes began.
- On Jan. 3, AP reported that the U.S. conducted a large-scale strike across Caracas, captured Maduro and Flores, and flew them out of the country.
