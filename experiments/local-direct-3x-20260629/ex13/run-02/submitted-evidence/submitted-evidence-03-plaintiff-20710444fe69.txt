Title: Iran has not yet decided on attending the next round of indirect talks with the U.S.: Spokesperson.

Relevant extracted text:
- Foreign Ministry spokesperson Esmaeil Baqaei said Tehran's decision to attend the next round of negotiations with the United States remained unclear.
- Speaking to IRIB about Iran's decision to attend the sixth round of talks with the United States in Muscat, Baqaei said it was inconceivable for Iran to imagine Israeli warmongering without U.S. coordination or deliberate approval.
- He said the other party acted in a way that effectively rendered negotiations meaningless.
- He said Iran held the U.S. administration responsible for the consequences of the Israeli regime's action.
- The report notes that Baqaei had earlier said on June 10 that Iran and the United States would hold the newest round of talks in Muscat on June 15 with Oman as mediator.
- It also notes that Iran and the United States had held five rounds of talks mediated by Omani Foreign Minister Sayyid Badr Al-Busaidi.
